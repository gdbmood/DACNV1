General Questions

What is DACN Technologies?

DACN Technologies is a post-quantum digital asset custody platform that provides governments and financial institutions with sovereign, provable, and quantum-secure control over digital assets. We integrate air-gapped key management, post-quantum cryptography (PQC), zero-knowledge proofs, and immutable audit trails to deliver institutional-grade security that meets the highest regulatory and operational standards.

Why is post-quantum cryptography important now?

Quantum computers will eventually break RSA and elliptic curve cryptography (ECC),the algorithms securing today's digital infrastructure. Adversaries are already conducting "Harvest Now, Decrypt Later" (HNDL) attacks, capturing encrypted data today to decrypt once quantum computers become available. With PQC standards published by MIST (FIPS 203-205) in August 2024 and government transition directives and roadmaps are active (OMB M-23-02, CNSA 2.0, EU Coordinated PQC Roadmap), the time to act is now, not when quantum computers arrive. More importantly, this is a fiduciary risk: if assets are compromised due to known quantum-vulnerable controls, liability sits with management and the board for failing to act on a foreseeable threat.

When will quantum computers threaten current cryptography?

Most expert outlooks place cryptographically relevant quantum computers capable of breaking the current RSA / ECDSA in the early-to-mid 2030s, but timelines remain uncertain, and could be earlier. The risk is already present via Harvest-Now-Decrypt-Later. Data captured today will be vulnerable once quantum computers mature, making current data protection a pressing concern. Recent incidents have already shown over $2.17 billion stolen from crypto services in 2025 YTD, surpassing all of 2024.

What are DACN Notes?

DACN Notes are phygital (physical + digital) bearer-style custody instruments that represent control over specific positions in DACN custody. Each note contains: A unique note identifier and metadata Linkage to underlying on-chain assets (vaults, UTXOs, accounts) An embedded shard of key material (where applicable) Post-quantum signatures (ML-DSA/SLH-DSA) binding all fields together DACN Notes can be stored in physical vaults like traditional bearer instruments while maintaining full cryptographic security and auditability.

What is AirGuard Custody?

AirGuard Custody is DACN's core, air-gapped custody engine responsible for: Secure key generation, storage, and lifecycle management for both classical and PQC keys Enforcing threshold schemes and approval policies (configurable 2-of-3, 3-of-5, 4-of-7, etc.) Executing offline signing operations in controlled, physically isolated environments AirGuard Custody runs in secure, offline environments and never exposes full private keys or threshold secrets outside the air-gapped perimeter.

What is the AirGuard Orchestration Module?

The AirGuard Orchestration Module is the online component that coordinates workflows including: Transaction preparation and policy enforcement Multi-party approvals and risk checks Integration with core banking, treasury, ERP, and trading systems The orchestration module communicates with AirGuard Custody through a strictly limited interface and never receives full private keys or sufficient information to reconstruct them. Orchestration keeps transaction preparation and approvals fully online and normal-speed; only the final signing step (and other key-sensitive operations) occurs inside the air-gapped perimeter.

What cryptographic algorithms does DACN use?

DACN implements NIST-standardized post-quantum algorithms: ML-KEM-768 (FIPS 203): Key encapsulation for secure key exchange ML-DSA-65 (FIPS 204): Digital signatures for authentication SLH-DSA-SHA2-192s (FIPS 205): Hash-based signatures for long-term security We support hybrid modes combining post-quantum with classical algorithms (RSA-4096, ECDSA P-384) during the transition period. Higher security profiles (ML-KEM-1024, ML-DSA-87, SLH-DSA-256s) are available for sovereign-grade and ultra-high-security deployments.

Why doesn't DACN use the highest security parameters everywhere?

DACN's default profiles (ML-KEM-768, ML-DSA-65, SLH-DSA-192s) balance security and performance for institutional workloads, aligning with AES-192 equivalent security levels. These parameters: Provide robust quantum resistance for the foreseeable threat horizon Optimize performance for custody operations Reduce storage and bandwidth requirements for DACN Notes and evidence packs Higher security profiles (ML-KEM-1024, ML-DSA-87, SLH-DSA-256s) are available and can be configured per vault for sovereign entities, systemically important institutions, or specific high-value use cases requiring maximum security margins.

What is "crypto-agility" and why does it matter?

Crypto-agility is the ability to upgrade or replace cryptographic algorithms without requiring a complete system redesign. This matters because PQC standards will evolve; crypto-agility ensures you are never locked into a single algorithm choice and can migrate without redesigning custody operations. DACN's modular architecture allows seamless transitions to new algorithms as standards evolve, with all changes captured in evidence packs. This ensures: Long-term security as PQC standards mature, Compliance with future regulatory requirements, Documented, auditable migration paths, No vendor lock-in to static algorithm choices.

How does DACN handle threshold schemes and key sharding?

DACN uses configurable threshold schemes (such as 2-of-3, 3-of-5, 4-of-7) where keys are split into multiple shards. Each shard is cryptographically useless on its own,only when the required threshold is met can operations be authorized. Our current implementation uses Shamir secret sharing with offline key generation, manual quorum control, and PQC wrappers. We're actively monitoring NIST's Multi-Party Threshold Cryptography project and will integrate formal threshold ML-DSA/ML-KEM protocols as they mature and standardize.

How does DACN protect against Harvest Now, Decrypt Later (HNDL) attacks?

DACN deploys hybrid post-quantum cryptography immediately, protecting: All DACN-controlled surfaces (control channels, DACN Notes, evidence packs) Key distribution and shard transport using ML-KEM Configuration manifests and critical artifacts with ML-DSA/SLH-DSA signatures Archive re-wrapping services to convert historical data to post-quantum protection Even if attackers record everything today and gain a CRQC tomorrow, DACN-controlled surfaces remain protected.

What is zero-knowledge (ZK) compliance?

Zero-knowledge proofs allow institutions to verify customer identities and prove regulatory compliance without exposing sensitive personal data. Applications include: Compliance: Prove a customer is "over 18" or "not on sanctions lists" without revealing actual identity details, Proof-of-Reserves: Demonstrate total assets exceed liabilities without revealing individual wallet addresses, Privacy-Preserving Billing: Calculate and prove average Assets under Custody (AuC) for billing purposes without storing raw per-address balances or transaction histories. This reduces data breach risk while maintaining full regulatory compliance and alignment with GDPR data minimization principles.

Will post-quantum cryptography affect performance?

Hybrid PQ implementations add minimal overhead, benchmarked at less than 10% for most operations: TLS handshakes: ~17% latency increase (negligible for custody operations), Transaction signing: Within acceptable bounds for high-value flows, Archive re-wrapping: ~10% throughput impact (I/O bound). DACN is optimized for high-value, governance-heavy flows rather than high-frequency trading. The orchestration module can scale horizontally for higher-volume operations while keeping the custody core secure and tightly controlled.

What about key compromise or loss scenarios?

Your threshold policy defines both security margin and recoverability; DACN enforces it exactly. DACN's threshold schemes provide resilience: Recoverable Cases: Loss or compromise of fewer than threshold shards may be addressed by disabling affected shards, rotating keys, and re-issuing DACN Notes with full evidence capture. Non-Recoverable Cases: If sufficient shards are irretrievably lost beyond the configured threshold, loss is cryptographically final by design. This is an intentional property preventing unilateral asset seizure or arbitrary intervention. All incident responses are captured in evidence packs with rationale, timestamps, and authorizations for regulatory review.
